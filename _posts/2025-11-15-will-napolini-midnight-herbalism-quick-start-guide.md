### ESSENTIAL TOOLS AND ACCESSORIES

First things first, let's talk about your tools. In *Midnight*, they are more critical than ever. It's not just about leveling upâ€”high-quality tools are essential for saving gold and maximizing your long-term earnings.

**A high-quality tool provides three crucial benefits:**

1. It boosts your **SKILL**, which directly determines the quality of the items you produce.
2. It provides a chance to save reagents on every craft, making your precious materials go further.
3. It increases **the speed of your gathering and the yield** you collect from Herbalism nodes.

Because of this, acquiring the best tools you can afford is a top priority. Think of it as a foundational investment that pays dividends every time you gather.

There are 5 different types of empowered herbs you will come across while leveling Herbalism. These special nodes provide additional benefits beyond standard gathering.

#### 1. Lush Herbs 

Herbs that have had plenty of resources (or some magical assistance) to grow and thrive beyond their normal means. Gathering them yields more resources than normal.

**Affected Herbs:**
- [Lush Tranquility Bloom](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1223148/lush-tranquility-bloom)

### SKILL 1 - 100

#### 1 - 25 (Eversong Woods)

Farm herbs in Eversong Woods until skill 25 to unlock Specializations. Then, choose Botany and put 40 Knowledge Points into it to gather while mounted.

> ğŸ’¡ **Note:** You can earn 44 bonus Knowledge Points early on: 24 from collecting 8 [Herbalism Treasures]() and 20 from collecting your weekly 5 [Thalassian Phoenix Tail](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/item=238466/thalassian-phoenix-tail).

#### 25 - 50 (Zul'aman)

Farm herbs in Zul'aman until you reach skill 50 to unlock your second Specialization. Then, choose [Bountiful Harvests](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/profession-trait/bountiful-harvests-104481) and allocate 5 Knowledge Points into it to unlock the sub-specialization, where you should select [Bloom Bringer](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/profession-trait/bloom-bringer-104480).

**Why?** This will allow you to gather more [Tranquility Bloom](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/item=236761/tranquility-bloom), which is heavily used for [Empowered Mulch](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1221181/empowered-mulch) and [Composite Flora](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1230855/composite-flora). [Composite Flora](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1230855/composite-flora) is required for the [Thalassian Alchemist's Mixcap](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1237550/thalassian-alchemists-mixcap) and various [Evergreen decor items](), such as the [Silvermoon Spire Fountain](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/spell=1233138/silvermoon-spire-fountain). This high demand makes [Tranquility Bloom](https://www.wowhead.com/beta/item=236761/tranquility-bloom) more valuable than any other herb in the Midnight expansion.

> ğŸ’¡ **Note:** All herbs are valuable, but some are more valuable than others. Prices depend on supply and demand; if the market is flooded with an herb, its price will drop. Generally, the greater the scarcity, the higher the price. =)

#### 50 - 75 (Harandar)

Farm herbs in Harandar until you reach skill 75 to unlock your third Specialization.

However, do not spend any Knowledge Points until all other specializations are complete. 



#### 75 - 100 (Voidstorm)

From levels 75 to 100, explore Voidstorm to reach the level cap and become familiar with all Midnight zones. You can then choose your favorite one to farm.
